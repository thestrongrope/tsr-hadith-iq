# Abaqat al-Anwar — Volume 7 Response Analysis

**Hadith defended:** Hadith of Ghadir Khumm
**Section:** Sanad
**Analyzed by:** Gemini 3.1 Pro

## Overview
This volume is entirely dedicated to the Sanad (chains of transmission) of the Hadith of Ghadir Khumm. The author compiles a massive, exhaustive list of prominent Sunni scholars, hadith masters, historians, and exegetes who have narrated, authenticated, and relied upon the hadith. The primary goal is to prove the absolute Tawatur (mass-transmission) of the hadith, refuting any Sunni polemical attempts (such as those by Fakhr al-Razi) to downgrade it to an isolated report (Khabar Wahid) in order to evade its binding theological implications for Imamate.

## Responses to Dehlavi (1)

### Response 1: Hadith of Ghadir Khumm: Dehlavi accepts the hadith as authentic but completely rejects the Shia linguistic and contextual interpretation.
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** While Dehlavi nominally accepts the hadith's authenticity, the broader Sunni polemical tradition (which Dehlavi builds upon) often attempts to downgrade its epistemological weight by classifying it as an isolated report (Ahad), as explicitly stated by Fakhr al-Razi. The author responds by establishing the undeniable Tawatur (mass-transmission) of the hadith. By proving that it was narrated by over a hundred companions and recorded by dozens of the highest Sunni authorities across all centuries, the author forces the opponent to accept it as Qat'i al-Sudur (absolutely certain in its issuance), which is a prerequisite for establishing foundational theological doctrines like Imamate.
**Evidence:** Fakhr al-Razi's admission in 'Mafatih al-Ghayb' and 'Nihayat al-Uqul', Al-Dhahabi's dedicated work compiling the routes of Ghadir and his authentications in 'Tadhkirat al-Huffaz', Al-Jazari's explicit declaration of its Tawatur in 'Asna al-Matalib', Al-Suyuti's declaration of its Tawatur, Shah Waliullah Dehlavi's narration of it in 'Qurrat al-Aynayn' and 'Izalat al-Khafa'

## Biographical Dossiers (4)

- **Fakhr al-Din al-Razi** — Highly praised as the Imam of his time in theology and exegesis, proving that his admission of the hadith's narration carries immense weight.
  Sources: Mir'at al-Janan by Al-Yafi'i, Wafayat al-A'yan by Ibn Khallikan, Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya by Al-Subki
- **Diya al-Din al-Maqdisi** — Described as a definitive Hujjah (proof), Hafiz, and the Sheikh of the Sunnah in the Levant, validating his inclusion of the hadith in his 'Al-Ahadith al-Mukhtara'.
  Sources: Tadhkirat al-Huffaz by Al-Dhahabi, Al-Ibar by Al-Dhahabi, Tabaqat al-Huffaz by Al-Suyuti
- **Yahya b. Sharaf al-Nawawi** — Revered as Sheikh al-Islam and a master of hadith and jurisprudence, validating his authentication of the hadith in 'Tahdhib al-Asma wa al-Lughat'.
  Sources: Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya by Ibn Qadi Shuhba, Tabaqat al-Huffaz by Al-Suyuti
- **Muhibb al-Din al-Tabari** — Acknowledged as the Sheikh of the Hijaz and a great scholar of traditions, validating his extensive narrations of Ghadir in 'Al-Riyad al-Nadira' and 'Dhakha'ir al-Uqba'.
  Sources: Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya by Al-Isnawi

## Prior Rebuttals Cited (3)

- **Shams al-Din al-Dhahabi** in *Tadhkirat al-Huffaz / Dedicated treatise on Ghadir*: Refuted the claim that the hadith is weak or isolated by compiling its numerous authentic routes.
- **Shams al-Din al-Jazari** in *Asna al-Matalib fi Manaqib Ali b. Abi Talib*: Explicitly declared the hadith to be Mutawatir from Ali and from the Prophet, dismissing those who attempt to weaken it as ignorant.
- **Jalal al-Din al-Suyuti** in *Al-Jami' al-Saghir / Tarikh al-Khulafa*: Explicitly classified the Hadith of Ghadir as Mutawatir.
