# Abaqat al-Anwar — Volume 20 Response Analysis

**Hadith defended:** Hadith of Thaqalayn
**Section:** Dalalah and Sanad (Refutation of Counter-Hadiths)
**Analyzed by:** Gemini 3.1 Pro

## Overview
This volume focuses on defending the Hadith of Thaqalayn. The author first refutes Dehlavi's claim that the hadith was solely narrated by Zayd b. Arqam by listing 34 Sahaba who narrated it. He then proves the hadith implies Imamate (Dalalah) by analyzing terms like 'Thaqalayn', 'Tamassuk', and 'Khalifatayn' using Sunni sources. Finally, he systematically dismantles Dehlavi's counter-arguments which relied on three Sunni hadiths: 'Follow the Sunnah of the Khulafa', 'Take half your religion from Humayra', and 'Be guided by Ammar', proving them to be either weak, fabricated, or actually supportive of the Shia position.

## Responses to Dehlavi (5)

### Response 1: Dehlavi claimed that the Hadith of Thaqalayn was only narrated by Zayd bin Arqam, attempting to downplay its mass-transmission.
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author lists 34 companions (Sahaba) who narrated the Hadith of Thaqalayn, including Ali, Hasan, Salman, Abu Dharr, Ibn Abbas, Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, and Jabir. He quotes numerous Sunni scholars like al-Sakhawi, al-Samhudi, and Ibn Hajar al-Makki who explicitly acknowledge its mass-transmission (Tawatur) and multitude of chains.
**Evidence:** Al-Sakhawi's Istijlab Irtiqa' al-Ghuraf, Al-Samhudi's Jawahir al-Iqdayn, Ibn Hajar al-Makki's Al-Sawa'iq al-Muhriqah

### Response 2: Dehlavi accepts the hadith but argues Sunnis are its true practitioners, implying it does not prove Shia Imamate or absolute obedience.
**Type:** DALALAH_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author provides over 60 'Wajh' (aspects/arguments) proving the hadith mandates absolute obedience and Imamate. He analyzes the words 'Tamassuk' (holding fast), 'I'tisam' (seeking refuge), and 'Khalifatayn' (two successors), quoting Sunni scholars who state that holding fast to the Ahl al-Bayt means following their rulings, guidance, and leadership, which is synonymous with Imamate.
**Evidence:** Al-Taftazani's Sharh al-Maqasid, Al-Qari's Mirqat Mafatih, Al-Munawi's Fayd al-Qadir, Al-Zarqani's Sharh al-Mawahib

### Response 3: Dehlavi countered Thaqalayn with the Sunni hadith: 'Follow my Sunnah and the Sunnah of the rightly guided caliphs (Khulafa)'.
**Type:** BIOGRAPHICAL_DOSSIER
**Author's response:** The author attacks the Sanad of this hadith, showing it is not in the Sahihayn and its narrators (like Khalid b. Ma'dan, Thawr b. Yazid, and Baqiyyah b. al-Walid) are weak, fabricators, or Nasibis from Homs who hated Ali. He also argues that even if authentic, the 'Khulafa' refers to the Ahl al-Bayt, as the Prophet explicitly called them 'Khalifatayn' in authentic versions of Thaqalayn.
**Evidence:** Al-Dhahabi's Mizan al-I'tidal, Ibn Hajar's Tahdhib al-Tahdhib

### Response 4: Dehlavi countered Thaqalayn with the hadith: 'Take half your religion from this Humayra (Aisha)'.
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author proves this hadith is a known fabrication (Mawdu'). He quotes major Sunni Hadith masters who explicitly state it has no chain of transmission and is a fabricated lie.
**Evidence:** Al-Mizzi and Al-Dhahabi (quoted via Ibn Kathir), Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani's Fath al-Bari, Al-Sakhawi's Al-Maqasid al-Hasanah, Ibn al-Jawzi's Al-Mawdu'at

### Response 5: Dehlavi countered Thaqalayn with the hadith: 'Be guided by the guidance of Ammar'.
**Type:** CONTRADICTION_EXPOSURE
**Author's response:** The author uses Ilzam to show that Ammar was a staunch, devoted follower of Ali who fought against the Sunni caliphs and Muawiyah. He cites historical accounts showing Umar rejected Ammar's hadith on Tayammum, Uthman had Ammar beaten, and Muawiyah killed him at Siffin. Therefore, following Ammar's guidance means following Ali and rejecting his opponents.
**Evidence:** Ibn Qutaybah's Al-Imamah wa al-Siyasah, Al-Tabari's Tarikh, Ibn al-Athir's Al-Kamil fi al-Tarikh, Ahmad b. Hanbal's Musnad

## Biographical Dossiers (4)

- **Thawr b. Yazid (ثور بن يزيد)** — Negative. A Nasibi from Homs whose grandfather was killed at Siffin fighting for Muawiyah. He openly hated Ali. He was also a Qadari (believed in free will against Sunni orthodoxy) and was criticized by Malik, Al-Awza'i, and Yahya al-Qattan.
  Sources: Mizan al-I'tidal, Tahdhib al-Tahdhib, Umdat al-Qari
- **Khalid b. Ma'dan (خالد بن معدان)** — Negative. He was the chief of police for the tyrant Yazid b. Muawiyah and a Nasibi from Homs. The author argues a chief of police for Yazid cannot be trusted in religion.
  Sources: Tahdhib al-Tahdhib, Dhayl al-Mudhayyal
- **Baqiyyah b. al-Walid (بقية بن الوليد)** — Negative. A known Mudallis (concealer of defects) who narrated from weak narrators, unknown people, and liars, dropping their names to make the chains look authentic. Criticized heavily by Ibn al-Jawzi, Al-Dhahabi, Abu Hatim, and Ibn Hibban.
  Sources: Mizan al-I'tidal, Tahdhib al-Tahdhib, Al-Mawdu'at, Taqrib al-Tahdhib
- **Muawiyah b. Salih (معاوية بن صالح)** — Negative. He was the Qadi of Andalusia for the Umayyads. Criticized by Yahya al-Qattan and Abu Hatim, who said he cannot be used as proof.
  Sources: Mizan al-I'tidal, Tahdhib al-Tahdhib, Taqrib al-Tahdhib

## Prior Rebuttals Cited (2)

- **Al-Mizzi and Al-Dhahabi** in *Quoted in Ibn Kathir's Takhrij Ahadith Mukhtasar Ibn al-Hajib*: Refuted the existence of any chain (Sanad) for the hadith 'Take half your religion from Humayra'.
- **Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani** in *Fath al-Bari*: Stated he never saw the word 'Humayra' in any authentic hadith except the one about the Abyssinians playing, refuting the 'half your religion' hadith.
