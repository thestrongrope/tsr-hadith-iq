# Abaqat al-Anwar — Volume 17 Response Analysis

**Hadith defended:** Hadith of the Light (Nur)
**Section:** Sanad (Chains of Transmission)
**Analyzed by:** Gemini 3.1 Pro

## Overview
This volume is dedicated to refuting Shah Abdul Aziz Dehlavi's claim that the Hadith of Nur (which states that the Prophet Muhammad and Imam Ali were created from the same pre-eternal light) is a fabrication. Mir Hamid Husain systematically lists dozens of prominent Sunni scholars, Hadith masters, and historians who have narrated this hadith in their works. He provides extensive biographical dossiers (tarajim) for each scholar using Sunni Rijal sources to prove their reliability, thereby demonstrating that the hadith has multiple authentic chains and is widely accepted in classical Sunni literature.

## Responses to Dehlavi (1)

### Response 1: Hadith of the Light (Nur): Shia say: Ali and the Prophet were created from the same pre-eternal light, proving Ali's absolute superiority and right to succession. Dehlavi's attack: Declares it fabricated, naming specific liars in the chain.
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE | BIOGRAPHICAL_DOSSIER | COUNTER_CITATION
**Author's response:** The author refutes Dehlavi's claim of fabrication by demonstrating that the hadith is not reliant on a single weak chain. He compiles a massive list of highly authoritative Sunni scholars—spanning several centuries—who narrated the Hadith of Nur in their authentic collections. By establishing the impeccable Sunni credentials of these narrators through Sunni Rijal books, he proves that Dehlavi's dismissal is academically flawed and ignores the mass transmission (Tawatur/Istifadah) of the hadith in Sunni corpus.
**Evidence:** Fada'il al-Sahaba by Ahmad bin Hanbal, Tarikh Dimashq by Ibn Asakir, Manaqib by Ibn Maghazili, Kifayat al-Talib by Al-Ganji al-Shafi'i, Al-Riyad al-Nadirah by Muhibb al-Din al-Tabari, Fara'id al-Simtayn by Al-Hamawini

## Biographical Dossiers (5)

- **Ahmad bin Hanbal** — Highly positive. Established as the Imam of the Hanbali school and a foremost authority in Hadith, proving that his narration of the Hadith of Nur in 'Fada'il al-Sahaba' carries immense weight.
  Sources: Tarikh Baghdad, Tahdhib al-Kamal, Tabaqat al-Hanabilah
- **Ibn Asakir (Ali bin al-Hasan)** — Highly positive. Recognized as the Hafiz of the Levant and a meticulous Hadith master. His inclusion of the hadith in 'Tarikh Dimashq' is used as strong evidence of its Sunni transmission.
  Sources: Siyar A'lam al-Nubala, Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, Wafayat al-A'yan
- **Al-Ganji al-Shafi'i (Muhammad bin Yusuf)** — Positive. Validated as a reliable Shafi'i scholar and Hafiz, defending his work 'Kifayat al-Talib' against anti-Shia bias.
  Sources: Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, Various historical chronicles
- **Muhibb al-Din al-Tabari** — Positive. Confirmed as the Shaykh of the Haram in Mecca and a leading Shafi'i jurist and Hadith scholar.
  Sources: Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, Shadharat al-Dhahab
- **Al-Hamawini (Ibrahim bin Muhammad)** — Positive. Established as a great Hadith master (Hafiz) and Sufi Shaykh, validating his narrations in 'Fara'id al-Simtayn'.
  Sources: Tadhkirat al-Huffaz, Al-Durar al-Kaminah
