# Abaqat al-Anwar — Volume 5 Response Analysis

**Hadith defended:** Hadith of Ghadir (and Hadith of Thaqalayn)
**Section:** Sanad Criticism, Rijal Analysis, and Historical Context
**Analyzed by:** Gemini 3.1 Pro

## Overview
This volume focuses on defending the Hadith of Ghadir against the criticisms of Fakhr al-Din al-Razi and Shah Abdul Aziz Dehlavi, who relied on the jarh (criticism) of Al-Jahiz, Ibn Abi Dawud, and Abu Hatim al-Razi to weaken the hadith. The author systematically destroys the credibility of these three critics using Sunni Rijal sources, proving they were Nasibis, fabricators, or overly strict. He also refutes the historical claim that Imam Ali was in Yemen during the event of Ghadir by citing the Sihah Sittah, and attacks the credibility of Abu Hurayrah and Sufyan al-Thawri.

## Responses to Dehlavi (4)

### Response 1: Dehlavi (following Fakhr al-Razi) attacks the authenticity and consensus of Hadith Ghadir by citing the criticisms of Al-Jahiz, Ibn Abi Dawud, and Abu Hatim al-Razi.
**Type:** BIOGRAPHICAL_DOSSIER
**Author's response:** The author argues that these critics are entirely unreliable according to Sunni standards. Al-Jahiz was a known heretic (Zindiq) and Nasibi; Ibn Abi Dawud was declared a liar by his own father and other scholars; and Abu Hatim was known to be overly strict and anomalous in his Rijal criticism.
**Evidence:** Mizan al-I'tidal by Al-Dhahabi, Lisan al-Mizan by Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani, Siyar A'lam al-Nubala by Al-Dhahabi

### Response 2: Dehlavi claims that considering Al-Jahiz an enemy of Wilayah (a Nasibi) is a 'Shia invention' and a false accusation.
**Type:** COUNTER_CITATION
**Author's response:** The author provides extensive quotes from top Sunni scholars (Al-Khattabi, Al-Dhahabi, Ibn Hajar, Tha'lab, Al-Azhari) who explicitly declared Al-Jahiz a heretic, a fabricator of hadith, a mocker of Islam, and a Nasibi who wrote books defending the enemies of Ahl al-Bayt.
**Evidence:** Gharib al-Hadith by Al-Khattabi, Ta'wil Mukhtalaf al-Hadith by Ibn Qutaybah, Tahdhib al-Lughah by Al-Azhari

### Response 3: Dehlavi and Razi claim that Imam Ali was in Yemen during the Farewell Hajj and the event of Ghadir Khumm, making the Shia narrative impossible.
**Type:** CONTRADICTION_EXPOSURE
**Author's response:** The author exposes this as a blatant contradiction of the most authentic Sunni books. He cites Sahih Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, Sunan Abi Dawud, Sunan al-Nasa'i, and Sunan Ibn Majah, all of which explicitly record that Imam Ali returned from Yemen and joined the Prophet in Mecca for the Farewell Hajj before the event of Ghadir.
**Evidence:** Sahih Bukhari (Kitab al-Hajj), Sahih Muslim (Description of Hajjat al-Wada'), Sunan al-Nasa'i (Al-Khasa'is)

### Response 4: Dehlavi claims that Sunnis do not narrate faults about the Sahaba, and that only Shia fabricate such faults.
**Type:** CONTRADICTION_EXPOSURE
**Author's response:** The author dedicates a section to exposing the faults of Abu Hurayrah using exclusively Sunni sources. He shows that Umar b. al-Khattab beat Abu Hurayrah for stealing from the public treasury in Bahrain, called him an enemy of God, and threatened to exile him for fabricating hadiths. He also shows Aisha repeatedly rejecting and mocking Abu Hurayrah's narrations.
**Evidence:** Al-Iqd al-Farid by Ibn Abd Rabbih, Tarikh Ibn Kathir, Ayn al-Isabah by Al-Suyuti, Ta'wil Mukhtalaf al-Hadith by Ibn Qutaybah

## Biographical Dossiers (6)

- **Amr b. Bahr Al-Jahiz** — Extremely negative. Declared a Nasibi, a fabricator of hadith (including a fake Hadith of Fadak), a heretic (Zindiq), and someone who mocked Islamic tenets. The author compiles quotes from Tha'lab, Al-Khattabi, Ibn Qutaybah, and Al-Azhari to prove his complete lack of credibility.
  Sources: Mizan al-I'tidal, Lisan al-Mizan, Siyar A'lam al-Nubala, Al-Mughni, Al-Wafi bi al-Wafayat
- **Abu Bakr Abdullah b. Sulayman (Ibn Abi Dawud)** — Negative. Shown to be a fabricator and a Nasibi. The author highlights that his own father (Abu Dawud, author of the Sunan) called him a liar, and other scholars like Ibrahim al-Isbahani and Ibn Sa'id confirmed his fabrications and extreme hostility towards Imam Ali.
  Sources: Mizan al-I'tidal, Lisan al-Mizan, Siyar A'lam al-Nubala
- **Abu Hatim al-Razi** — Negative in the context of Jarh. The author cites Al-Dhahabi to prove that Abu Hatim was 'mut'annit' (overly strict and anomalous) in his criticism, to the point where he even attacked Imam Bukhari. Therefore, his weakening of Hadith Ghadir is worthless.
  Sources: Siyar A'lam al-Nubala, Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya
- **Abu Hurayrah** — Negative. The author compiles Sunni reports showing he was beaten by Umar for theft, threatened with exile for excessive and false narrations, and repeatedly corrected and called a liar by Aisha and Ibn Umar.
  Sources: Tarikh Ibn Kathir, Al-Iqd al-Farid, Ayn al-Isabah, Al-Mustadrak
- **Sufyan al-Thawri** — Negative. Proven to be a 'Mudallis' (one who deceptively conceals weak narrators in chains) and shown to have harbored hostility and disrespect towards Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq.
  Sources: Mizan al-I'tidal, Tahdhib al-Tahdhib, Taqrib al-Tahdhib, Lawaqih al-Anwar
- **Ibrahim b. Sayyar al-Nazzam** — Negative. The teacher of Al-Jahiz, described as a heretic, a drunkard, and someone who held corrupt beliefs, further invalidating the intellectual lineage of Al-Jahiz.
  Sources: Al-Wafi bi al-Wafayat
