Chapter 7: Shia Beliefs on Imamate

Pages 348-472 (125 pages)

## Overview

Dehlavi refutes Shia proofs for the Imamate of Imam Ali (as) in four sections:

1. **Sunni Imamate Theory** (pp 348-394) — 6 Sunni beliefs + defense of the three Caliphs
2. **Shia Quranic Proofs** (pp 394-421) — 6 verses Shia cite, each rebutted
3. **Shia Hadith Proofs** (pp 417-442) — 12 hadiths Shia cite, each rebutted
4. **Shia Rational Proofs** (pp 442-472) — 6 rational arguments + closing meta-argument

Dehlavi employs **7 rebuttal methods**: Logical reasoning; Meaning minimization; Counter-verses; Using Shia sources against Shia; Counter-hadiths; Grammatical/linguistic; Chain criticism (jarh)

## Section II: Shia Quranic Proofs

Dehlavi addresses 6 Quranic verses Shia cite for Imam Ali's Imamate:

**1. Ayat al-Wilayah** (5:55) — pp 394-404
> إِنَّمَا وَلِيُّكُمُ اللَّهُ وَرَسُولُهُ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا

Rebuttal: Logical reasoning, Grammatical/linguistic

**2. Ulu al-Amr** (4:59) — pp 408-411
> أَطِيعُوا اللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا الرَّسُولَ وَأُولِي الْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ

Rebuttal: Counter-hadiths, Logical reasoning

**3. Ayat al-Tathir** (33:33) — pp 404-410
> إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ اللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ الرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ الْبَيْتِ

Rebuttal: Grammatical/linguistic, Logical reasoning

**4. Ayat al-Mubahala** (3:61) — pp 411-414
> فَقُلْ تَعَالَوْا نَدْعُ أَبْنَاءَنَا وَأَبْنَاءَكُمْ

Rebuttal: Logical reasoning, Using Shia sources against Shia

**5. Ayat al-Mawaddah** (42:23) — pp 414-417
> قُل لَّا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلَّا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَىٰ

Rebuttal: Logical reasoning, Chain criticism (jarh)

**6. Ayat al-Sadiqin** (9:119) — pp 420-421
> كُونُوا مَعَ الصَّادِقِينَ

Rebuttal: Logical reasoning, Meaning minimization

*Note: Ayat al-Tabligh (5:67) and Ayat al-Ikmal (5:3), sometimes listed as Shia proofs for Imamate, are not discussed by Dehlavi in this chapter — verified against the OCR text.*

## Section III: Shia Hadith Proofs

Dehlavi addresses 12 hadiths Shia cite for Imam Ali's Imamate:

**1. Hadith al-Ghadir** (pp 417-422)
> من كنت مولاه فعلي مولاه

Dehlavi's rebuttal: مولی means friend, not ruler

**2. Hadith al-Manzilah** (pp 422-424)
> أنت مني بمنزلة هارون من موسى

Dehlavi's rebuttal: Specific to Tabuk. Harun died before Musa — no succession

**3. Hadith of Burayda** (pp 424-424)
> إن علياً مني وأنا من علي وهو ولي كل مؤمن

Dehlavi's rebuttal: Chain contains Ajlah — a weak Shia narrator

**4. Hadith al-Tayr** (pp 425-426)
> اللهم ائتني بأحب خلقك إليك

Dehlavi's rebuttal: Most scholars declare it fabricated

**5. Madinat al-Ilm** (pp 426-426)
> أنا مدينة العلم وعلي بابها

Dehlavi's rebuttal: "Gate of knowledge" does not imply caliphate

**6. Twelve Caliphs** (pp 426-431)
> يكون اثنا عشر أميراً كلهم من قريش

Dehlavi's rebuttal: Fits Sunni caliphs, not Shia Imams

**7. Man Nasaba Aliyyan** (pp 431-432)
> من ناصب علياً الخلافة فهو كافر

Dehlavi's rebuttal: نصب means "contested", not "denied"

**8. Hadith al-Nur** (pp 432-433)
> كنت أنا وعلي نوراً بين يدي الله

Dehlavi's rebuttal: Chain extremely weak. Pre-creation, not political

**9. Hadith al-Rayah** (pp 434-434)
> لأعطين الراية غداً رجلاً يحب الله ورسوله

Dehlavi's rebuttal: Describes love and bravery, not caliphate

**10. Adir al-Haqq** (pp 434-438)
> اللهم أدر الحق معه حيث دار

Dehlavi's rebuttal: If truth always follows Ali, it proves the caliphs were right too (since Ali endorsed them)

**11. Ta'wil** (pp 438-439)
> تقاتل على تأويل القرآن كما قاتلت على تنزيله

Dehlavi's rebuttal: Ali fought for interpretation — does not prove immediate caliphate

**12. Thaqalayn + Safinah** (pp 440-442)
> إني تارك فيكم الثقلين... كتاب الله وعترتي

Dehlavi's rebuttal: Holding fast means love and guidance, not political Imamate

### Hadith 12 in Detail: Thaqalayn + Safinah

Begins mid-page 439. Safinah introduced mid-page 440. This is what **Abaqat Volume 23** responds to.

**Safinah means love, not Imamate**

- Only Sunnis truly love ALL Ahl al-Bayt
- Shia love selectively — violates Q2:85
- Shia's own narrations agree with Sunnis

**Ship compartment reductio**

- If one compartment suffices, Zaydiyyah and Kaysaniyyah are also saved
- Twelver exclusivity collapses
- Accepting all sects as saved = affirming contradictories (impossible)
- Ship saves only if intact — Shia "make holes" by rejecting some Ahl al-Bayt

**Only the Sunni interpretation resolves rational objections**

- Following all Ahl al-Bayt is logically impossible (they disagree)
- Q5:48 — one shari'ah per ummah, not multiple valid paths

## Section IV: Shia Rational Proofs

Dehlavi selects 6 key rational arguments and adds a closing meta-argument:

- All Shia proofs reduce to 3 types — undermine the foundations and the superstructure falls
- Imam must be infallible — but infallibility cannot be known except through a prophet; creates circular reasoning
- Imam must never have worshipped idols — condition invented to exclude the three Caliphs
- Ali always complained about the Caliphs — but his complaints were political, not doctrinal
- Ali claimed Imamate and showed miracles — but he never claimed during the three Caliphs' era
- Ali had no disqualifying defects — but the Caliphs' alleged defects are fabricated

### Closing Argument (pp 462-472)

Shia sectarian splits disprove all tawatur claims. If even one faction's report were truly mutawatir, the disagreement would never have occurred. Names specific disputes: Muhammad ibn al-Hanafiyyah vs Zayn al-Abidin, Zayd vs Baqir, Ja'far vs Muhammad al-Mahdi. Charges that Imams were invented for financial gain. Cites Q37:69-70.
