## 2. Concordances and Indices (معاجم)

A **Mu'jam** (معجم, pl. Ma'ajim معاجم) is an alphabetical index of the words of a particular important book. It is used to find references for any quotation and is essential for research work. The summarized indices printed after the Holy Quran were called **Kashf al-Ayat**.

> **Tip:** Always read the preface of any *dalil* (index) for the method of usage.

#### Note on Ihqaq al-Haqq

*Ihqaq al-Haqq wa Izhaq al-Batil* (إحقاق الحق وإزهاق الباطل) by Qadi Nurullah Shushtari (القاضي الشهيد نور الله التستري) is a book on Imamat in 1 volume. It is the fourth in a series of Shia-Bakri debates:

1. **Nahj al-Haqq** (نهج الحق) — Allamah Hilli (ابن المطهر الحلي)
2. **Minhaj al-Sunnah** (منهاج السنة النبوية) — Ibn Taymiyyah
3. **Ibtal al-Batil** (إبطال نهج الباطل) — Fadl b. Ruzbahan (فضل الله بن روزبهان الخنجي)
4. **Ihqaq al-Haqq** — Qadi Nurullah Shushtari

## 3. Shia Tafaseer

In Shia sources, there are six types of tafaseer:

| Type | Arabic | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Riwai** | روائي | Contain only traditions. Referred to by Muhadditheen and Akhbariyyeen. |
| **Adabi** | أدبي | Literary; focus on grammatical meaning, Arabic poetry. |
| **Kalaami** | كلامي | Contain arguments and theological debates. |
| **Irfani / Falsafi** | عرفاني / فلسفي | Philosophical and mystical discussions. |
| **Ilmi** | علمي | Scientific arguments (social, political, experimental sciences). |
| **Ijtima'i** | اجتماعي | Social commentary. |

### 3.1 Riwaii Tafaseer (روائي)

#### 3.1.1 Tafsir al-Qummi (تفسير قمّي)

- **Author:** Ali b. Ibrahim b. Hashim al-Qummi (علي ابن ابراهيم قمّي), d. 306 AH
- **Volumes:** 2 (originally 1)
- **Significance:** Known as *Umm al-Tafasir* (the mother of all Tafaseer). A pure Shia tafseer.
- **Key details:**
  - Both Ali and his father Ibrahim were companions of Imam al-Naqi (a.s) and Imam al-'Askari (a.s). Hence several hadith do not require chains of narrators.
  - His grandfather was a companion of Imam al-Sadiq (a.s), originally from Kufa, migrated to Qum.
  - 2/3 of traditions mention names of Imams and narrators; 1/3 do not.
  - **Reliability:** Even his statements narrated without naming an Imam are accepted as traditions.
  - Syed Hashim al-Bahrani took his preface in its entirety as Preface no. 17 in *Tafsir al-Burhan*.
  - Among his students was Shaikh Muhammad b. Ya'qub al-Kulayni.

#### 3.1.2 Tafsir al-'Ayyashi (تفسير العياشي)

- **Author:** Abu al-Nasr Muhammad b. Mas'ud al-'Ayyashi al-Samarqandi, 3rd century AH
- **Volumes:** 2 (only through Surah al-Kahf survives — more than half destroyed by Bakris)
- **Key details:**
  - Bakri convert. Originally from Samarqand, settled in Baghdad.
  - His father left him 1,000 kg of gold, which he spent entirely on spreading traditions.
  - His house was divided into three sections: receiving guests, researching traditions, writing and publishing traditions.

#### 3.1.3 Tafsir Imam Hasan al-'Askari (a.s) (تفسير الإمام العسكري عليه السلام)

- **Narrators:** Yusuf b. Muhammad b. Ziyad and Ali b. Muhammad Sayyad
- **Content:** Only Surah al-Fatiha and 150 verses of Surah al-Baqarah
- **History:** Originally 120 volumes dictated over 7 years. Destroyed in the Baghdad riots of 450 AH; only 1 volume survives.
- **Controversy:** This tafsir is a major point of contention among Shia scholars.

| Shahid al-Awwal | Muhaqqiq Mir Damad |
| Shahid al-Thani | Muqaddas Ardabili |
| Muhammad Taqi Majlisi | Shaikh M. Jawad Balaghi Najafi |

#### 3.1.4 Tafsir Ibn Mahyar / Tafsir Ma Nazala fi al-Nabi wa Alihi

- **Author:** Ibn Mahyar (ابن ماهيار), d. 385 AH
- **Original size:** 1,000 pages (per Najashi)
- **Status:** Lost after 9th century. Syed Sharafuddin Istarabadi (d. 937 AH) preserved it in his *Ta'wil al-Ayat al-Bahira*.
- **Significance:** Major reference for Bihar, Nur al-Thaqalayn, and Tafsir al-Burhan. Contains extensive traditions on Imamat (e.g., 50 on Ayat al-Mubahala, 120 on chain of Surah Bara'at, 90 on Ayat al-Wilaya, 21 on Ayat al-Tabligh).

#### 3.1.5 Tafsir Jami' (تفسير جامع)

- **Author:** Syed Ibrahim Burujardi (سيد إبراهيم بروجردي) — Akhbari
- **Volumes:** 7
- **Content:** Compilation of Tafsir Imam 'Askari (a.s), Tafsir al-'Ayyashi, Kutub Arba', and Tafsir Ibrahim al-Qummi. Includes the relevance of each verse. Translation of Tafsir al-Qummi in footnotes.

#### 3.1.6 Tafsir Furat (تفسير فرات)

- **Author:** Furat b. Ibrahim al-Kufi (فرات بن إبراهيم الكوفي), 3rd century AH
- **Volumes:** 2
- **Content:** Only those verses relevant to the Ahl al-Bayt (a.s).

#### 3.1.7 Al-Nur al-Mubin fi Tafsir Amir al-Mu'minin (a.s)

- **Author:** Dr. Nadir Fadli (دكتور نادر فضلي)
- **Volumes:** 10 (vol. 1 is preface only)
- **Content:** Only ahadith related by Amir al-Mu'minin (a.s). PhD thesis at Tehran University; work ongoing.

#### 3.1.8 Tafsir Nur al-Thaqalayn (تفسير نور الثقلين)

- **Author:** Allamah Shaikh Abd al-Ali al-Huwaizi (عبد علي العروسي الحويزي), d. 1112 AH
- **Volumes:** 5
- **Content:** Only verses for which traditions were found. Contemporary of Allamah Majlisi.

#### 3.1.9 Tafsir al-Burhan (تفسير برهان)

- **Author:** Syed Hashim al-Bahrani (سيد هاشم بحراني)
- **Volumes:** Originally 4, currently 10
- **Highlight:** 18 prefaces — some researchers believe these were not all written by al-Bahrani. Includes a long preface by Shaikh al-Asifi. Preface 17 is the preface of Ali b. Ibrahim al-Qummi.

#### 3.1.10 Tafsir al-Safi (تفسير الصافي)

- **Author:** Mulla Muhsin Fayz al-Kashani (ملامحسن فيض كاشانى), d. ~1091 AH
- **Volumes:** Originally 2, now 5
- **Content:** Riwai and Irfani (mystical). Introduction in 12 chapters.
- **Persian translation:** *Rawan-i Jawid* (روان جاويد).
- **Note:** Fayz was among the greatest Muhaddith, Faqih, and philosopher. Son-in-law of Mulla Sadra. Influenced by Sufi ideas. Also wrote *al-Muhajja al-Bayda'* on Akhlaq. Summarized as *Asfa*, and further as *Musaffa*.

#### 3.1.11 Tafsir al-Ithna 'Ashari (تفسير الاثني عشري)

- **Author:** Shah 'Abd al-'Azimi (حسين بن أحمد الحسيني الشاه عبد العظيمي)
- **Volumes:** 14
- **Language:** Persian
- **Content:** Mostly Riwai with some Kalaami discussion; no philosophical discussions. Considered one of the best Persian tafaseer.

### 3.2 Adabi / Literary Tafaseer (أدبي)

#### 3.2.1 Tafsir al-Tibyan (تفسیر تبیان)

- **Author:** Shaikh Abu Ja'far Muhammad b. Hasan al-Tusi (محمد بن حسن طوسی), b. 385 AH, d. 460 AH
- **Volumes:** 10
- **Title:** Shaikh al-Ta'ifa
- **Significance:** First to discuss the Quran from the literary aspect, using Arabic grammar to derive meaning. Contains some Kalaami discussions.
- **Biography:** Born in Tus (Khurasan). Studied under Shaikh Mufid (408–413 AH) and Syed Murtada (413–426 AH, for 14 years). Syed Murtada was very wealthy (700 villages between Baghdad and Karbala) and gave Shaikh Tusi 20 gold coins monthly. After riots destroyed his house and library in Baghdad (containing 1,000 Shia books), he migrated to Najaf and established the Hawza-i 'Ilmiyyah.
- **Note:** Shaikh Buzurgh Tehrani's *al-Dhari'a* (26 vols.) is an encyclopedia of all Shia books.

#### 3.2.2 Tafsir Majma' al-Bayan (مجمع البيان في تفسير القرآن)

- **Author:** Shaikh Abu Ali Fadl b. Hasan al-Tabrisi (أبو علي الفضل بن الحسن الطبرسي), b. 468 AH, d. 548 AH (Mashhad)
- **Volumes:** Arabic: 10, Persian: 24

## 5. History and Development of Hadith

### 5.1 Periods of Hadith Compilation

At the time of the Holy Prophet (s.a.w.a.), most people were illiterate. The Prophet addressed this by making literate Muslims teach others.

**Bakri side:** From Abu Bakr's time until Umar b. 'Abd al-'Aziz (~100 years), writing and narrating true traditions was banned, while fabrication was allowed — the aim being to suppress the Manaqib of Imam Ali (a.s).

**Shia side:** Though in a minority and subject to persecution, followers of the Imams (a.s) continued writing and compiling traditions. However, many fabrications also entered due to forced migrations.

## 6. The Four Early Books (الكتب الأربعة المقدّمة)

Written by the "Three Muhammads" (*Muhammadun Thalatha*).

### 6.1 Al-Kafi

- **Author:** Shaikh Muhammad b. Ya'qub al-Kulayni, d. 329 AH (*'Am Tanathur al-Nujum* — the year of the falling stars)
- **Duration:** ~20 years to compile
- **Structure:** 34 books, 326 chapters, 16,121 traditions
  - Usul (2 vols.) — beliefs and principles
  - Furu' (5 vols.) — jurisprudence
  - Rawdah (1 vol.) — Wilayat and general topics
- **Significance:** More traditions than all of Sihah Sittah combined (~6,000 in Sihah Sittah)
- **Reliability debate:**
  - Muhaddith Nuri's position: All traditions reliable because (1) taken from Usul Arba' Mi'ah, (2) author was a Muhaddith, (3) he selected, not merely collected.
  - Ayatullah al-Khoei's position: Only ~9,000 traditions are Sahih; ~6,500 are weak. Blind belief is incorrect.
- **Commentaries:** 40+ Sharh written, most famous being *Mir'at al-'Uqul* by Allamah Majlisi (26 vols.)
- **Biography:** Born in Kulayn. Teachers: Ali b. Ibrahim al-Qummi. Students: Nu'mani, Harun Talla'akbari. Trained in the period from Imam al-Kazim (a.s) to the beginning of the Lesser Occultation.

### 6.2 Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih

- **Author:** Shaikh Muhammad b. Ali b. Babawayh — **Shaikh al-Saduq**, b. 305 AH, d. 381 AH
- **Statistics:** 5,916 traditions (2,000 are Mursal/without Sanad), 566 chapters
- **Significance:** Could be considered the first *Tawdih al-Masa'il*; Shaikh Saduq was the first Marja of the Shia world in the Greater Occultation.
- **Commentaries:** 20+ Sharh written
- **Biography:**
  - Born in Qum during the time of Husain b. Ruh (3rd Na'ib). His father asked the 12th Imam (a.t.f.s.) for a child through the 3rd Na'ib; Imam replied both sons would become Fuqaha.
  - Studied from his father for 20 years. Most famous teacher: Muhammad b. Hasan b. Walid.
  - From 339 AH, traveled for 8 years collecting traditions, meeting 211 Ulema.
  - Settled in Rayy at the request of the Buyid king Rukn al-Dawla (347 AH).
  - Students: Ibn Khazzaz Qummi, Harun Talla'akbari, Ibn al-Ghada'iri, father of Najashi, Shaikh Mufid.
  - 219 books recorded. *Madinat al-'Ilm* (10 vols.) was more comprehensive than Faqih but destroyed after the 11th century.
- **Other major works:**

| Book | Topic |
|------|-------|
| *Al-Khisal* | Traditions arranged by numbers |
| *Thawab al-A'mal wa 'Iqab al-A'mal* | Rewards and punishments |
| *Ma'ani al-Akhbar* | Meanings of terms used in traditions |
| *Al-Amali* | Miscellaneous dictations to students |
| *Al-Tawhid* | Tawhid and 'Adl |
| *Kamal al-Din* | About Imam al-Zaman (a.t.f.s.) |
| *'Ilal al-Shara'i'* | Reasons for jurisprudential rulings |
| *'Uyun Akhbar al-Rida* | Sayings and debates of Imam Rida (a.s) |
| *Sifat al-Shia* & *Fada'il al-Shia* | Characteristics and virtues of Shia |

### 6.3 Tahdhib al-Ahkam

- **Author:** Shaikh Abu Ja'far Muhammad b. Hasan al-Tusi — **Shaikh al-Ta'ifa**, b. Ramadan 385 AH, d. 22 Muharram 460 AH
- **Content:** Sharh of *al-Muqni'a* by Shaikh Mufid
- **Statistics:** 393 chapters, 13,590 traditions. 16+ commentaries written.
- **Duration:** 40 years of work
- **Significance:** The primary book of Ijtihad and Fiqh before *Wasa'il al-Shia*. 10 volumes.
- **Biography:**
  - Born in Tus (Khurasan). Migrated to Baghdad in 408 AH.
  - Studied under Shaikh Mufid (5 years), then Syed Murtada (23 years).
  - More than 300 Mujtahidin were his students.
  - The Abbasid Caliph al-Qa'im bi Amrillah offered him the chair of theology.
  - In 448 AH, his library was burnt; he migrated to Najaf and established the Hawza.
  - **50+ books** including: *al-Nihaya*, *al-Khilaf* (first comparative Fiqh), *al-Mabsut*, *al-'Udda*, *al-Fihrist*, *al-Rijal*, *Talkhis al-Shafi*, *Misbah al-Mutahajjid*, *Tafsir al-Tibyan*, *al-Ghaybah*, *al-Amali*.

### 6.4 Al-Istibsar

- **Author:** Shaikh al-Tusi (same as above)
- **Content:** Summary of Tahdhib, focused on resolving conflicting traditions
- **Statistics:** 4 volumes, 925 chapters, 5,511 traditions. 18 commentaries written.

> **Note on reliability (Ayatullah al-Khoei):** In *Mu'jam Rijal al-Hadith* (vol. 1, pp. 22–36), he discusses the authenticity of the Kutub Arba' and warns against blind acceptance. Some traditions accepted in one book are rejected in another (e.g., Shaikh Tusi rejected in Tahdhib some traditions brought by Shaikh Saduq in Faqih).

## 7. The Four Later Books (الكتب الأربعة المؤخّرة)

### 7.1 Al-Wafi

- **Author:** Mulla Muhammad b. Murtada — **Fayz al-Kashani**, b. 1007 AH, d. 1091 AH
- **Title:** 'Alam al-Huda
- **Content:** Summarized all Kutub Arba' with explanations of traditions
- **Statistics:** 14 vols., 79 chapters, 2,083 sub-chapters, ~40,000 traditions
- **Related works:** *Al-Shafi* (summary of al-Wafi), *al-Nawadir* (similar but from non-Kutub Arba' sources)
- **Teachers:** Mulla Sadra, Mir Damad, Shaikh Baha'i. Also son-in-law of Mulla Sadra.
- **Students:** Allamah Muhammad Baqir Majlisi, Syed Ni'matullah al-Jaza'iri
- **Note:** 150+ books. Started as a philosopher, then became a mystic, then repented from both. His *al-Kalimat al-Maknuna* remains a Sufi reference.

### 7.2 Bihar al-Anwar

- **Author:** Allamah Muhammad Baqir Majlisi, d. 1111 AH (buried in Isfahan next to his father)
- **Content:** Comprehensive Shia encyclopedia based on Quran and traditions
- **Statistics:** Collected from 393 books (beyond Kutub Arba'), 2,489 chapters, 110 volumes (originally 25)
- **Organization:** Volumes 54–56 (Iran ed.) / 108–110 (Lebanon ed.) = Index

- **Selective approach:** Majlisi did *not* include traditions from some books he considered unreliable (e.g., *Mashariq Anwar al-Yaqin* of Hafiz Bursi due to Ghuluww; *Misbah al-Shari'a* attributed to Sufis; *Awali al-La'ali* and *Tanbih al-Khawatir*).
- **Other works:** *Mir'at al-'Uqul* (Sharh of Kafi, 23+ vols.), *Maladh al-Akhyar* (Sharh of Tahdhib), *Haqq al-Yaqin*, *Tuhfat al-Za'ir*, *Zad al-Ma'ad*, Rijal al-Majlisi.

### 7.3 Wasa'il al-Shia

- **Author:** Allamah Muhammad b. Hasan al-Hurr al-'Amili, b. Rajab 1033 AH, d. 1104 AH
- **Origin:** Jabal 'Amil, Lebanon
- **Statistics:** 42 books, 187 chapters, 7,065 sub-chapters, 35,843 traditions
- **Duration:** 20 years to compile
- **Sources:** Directly from 180 books. Lists 1,487 narrators, all considered authentic by the author.
- **Editions:** Originally 9 vols. → 20 vols. (by order of Ayatullah Burujardi, under Rabbani Shirazi) → 30 vols. (with additional references)
- **Other works:** *Ithbat al-Hudat*, *Fusul al-Muhimma*, *al-Iqaz min al-Haja'at*, *al-Jawahir al-Saniyyah*, *Amal al-Amil*, *Bidayat al-Hidaya* (1,535 Wajibat + 1,448 Muharramat).

### 7.4 Mustadrak al-Wasa'il

- **Author:** Allamah Mirza Husain b. Muhammad Taqi Nuri al-Mazandarani — **Khatam al-Muhaddithin**, b. 1254 AH, d. 1320 AH
- **Statistics:** 23,129 traditions (only those not in Wasa'il), from 70 additional books. Originally 3 vols., now 18 vols.
- **Biography:** Born in village of Yalu in city of Nur. Orphan at age 8. Migrated to Iraq in 1273 AH. Studied under Shaikh 'Abd al-Husain Tehrani, Shaikh Murtada Ansari, Syed Mujaddid Shirazi. He knew all traditions and their asnad by heart.
- **Other works:** *Nafas al-Rahman fi Ahwal Salman*, *Kashf al-Astar*, *Najm al-Thaqib*, *al-Fayd al-Qudsi*, *Dar al-Salam* (4 vols. on dreams), *Jannat al-Ma'wa* (100 incidents of meeting Imam al-Zaman), *Ma'alim al-Abrar*.
- **Controversial work:** *Fasl al-Khitab fi Tahrif al-Kitab* — Ayatullah Burujardi ordered its destruction.

## 9. Bakri Hadith Collections

### Historical Context

From Abu Bakr's time, narration of traditions was banned for ~140 years. The ban was lifted during the caliphate of Umar b. 'Abd al-'Aziz. During this period, fabrication of false traditions was rampant — the aim was to suppress Ahl al-Bayt (a.s).

- **Abu Hanifa** claimed only 17 traditions in all Bakri books are correct.
- **Malik** said only 30 books are correct.
- **Bukhari** selected only 6,000 from 600,000 traditions.
- **Suyuti:** "Nothing authentic exists in the virtues of Mu'awiyah" (لا يصح فى فضلة معاوية).

### Critiques of Sihah Sittah

1. Not all traditions are Sahih — at least 374 narrators are Fasiq, Majhul, or Kadhdhab (per Tabari in *Dala'il al-Sidq*).
2. Many other books also claim to contain correct traditions.
3. If these were "complete," why write Mustadrak?
4. Many Bakri-accepted traditions are not found in the Sihah.
5. Traditions accepted by Bukhari may be rejected by Muslim and vice versa.
6. The authors themselves never claimed all traditions were correct.
