Dehlavi's Strategy

Dehlavi accepts both Thaqalayn and Safinah as authentic hadiths but reinterprets their meaning to deny exclusive Shia Imamate. He deploys counter-hadiths (some fabricated), reductio arguments, and sectarian charges.

Key finding: Of the hadiths he cites as evidence, one is graded FABRICATED by Sunni hadith scholars, one is OVERSTATED in grade, and he names ZERO source books for any hadith.

Overall Assessment

Evidentiary quality: Mixed: Accepts core Thaqalayn (sahih confirmed); accurate on some counter-hadiths (e.g., Mu'adh sahih); fabricates/overstates others (A'isha mawḍūʿ, Abu Bakr hasan→tawātur); Safinah not in Sunnah.com but Sunni-cited. No specific book citations.

Logical quality: Sophisticated reductios and tu quoques; vulnerable to specific Ahl al-Bayt definitions and dalālah hierarchy.

Key weakness: Overbroad 'itrah/Ahl al-Bayt = all relatives; fabricated hadiths undermine counters.

Key strength: Exposes tensions in partial adherence without clear criteria.
