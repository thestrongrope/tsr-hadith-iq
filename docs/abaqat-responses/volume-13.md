# Abaqat al-Anwar — Volume 13 Response Analysis

**Hadith defended:** Hadith of the Roasted Bird (Tayr)
**Section:** Sanad and Dalalah
**Analyzed by:** Gemini 3.1 Pro

## Overview
This volume is a comprehensive defense of the Hadith of the Roasted Bird (Hadith al-Tayr). The author systematically refutes Shah Abdul Aziz Dehlavi's claims that the hadith is fabricated (Mawdu') or weak. He provides an exhaustive list of 86 prominent Sunni scholars and traditionists who narrated the hadith, proving its mutawatir status. He refutes the weakening of the hadith by Ibn al-Jawzi, Ibn Taymiyyah, and Al-Dhahabi by citing other major Sunni authorities (like Al-Suyuti and Ibn Hajar) who authenticated it and criticized Ibn al-Jawzi's leniency in declaring hadiths fabricated. Furthermore, he defends the Dalalah (meaning) of the hadith, refuting Dehlavi's attempt to restrict the meaning of 'most beloved of Your creation' to merely 'most beloved in eating this bird', providing 70 rational and linguistic arguments against this restriction. He also refutes Dehlavi's use of the counter-hadith 'Iqtadu bil-ladhayni min ba'di' (Follow the two after me).

## Responses to Dehlavi (4)

### Response 1: Hadith of the Roasted Bird (Tayr): Shia say: Ali is the most beloved person to God, and the most beloved must be the Imam. Dehlavi's attack: Strongly attacks authenticity, citing Al-Dhahabi and Al-Jazari who classify it as fabricated.
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author proves the hadith is Mutawatir by listing dozens of Sahaba who narrated it and 86 major Sunni scholars who recorded it in their authentic books. He shows that major Sunni hadith masters (like Al-Suyuti, Ibn Hajar, and Al-Sakhawi) rejected Ibn al-Jawzi's and Al-Dhahabi's classification of it as Mawdu' (fabricated). He even lists 14 Sunni scholars who wrote independent treatises solely on the Hadith of the Bird.
**Evidence:** Sunan al-Tirmidhi, Khasa'is Amir al-Mu'minin by Al-Nasa'i, Al-Mustadrak by Al-Hakim, Al-La'ali al-Masnu'a by Al-Suyuti

### Response 2: Ibn Taymiyyah's claim (adopted by Dehlavi) that the hadith is not found in the Sihah or reliable Sunni books.
**Type:** CONTRADICTION_EXPOSURE
**Author's response:** The author exposes this as a blatant lie by quoting the hadith directly from Al-Tirmidhi, Al-Nasa'i, and other major Sunni collections, proving Ibn Taymiyyah's extreme bias and lack of reliability in hadith sciences.
**Evidence:** Minhaj al-Sunnah by Ibn Taymiyyah, Sunan al-Tirmidhi, Khasa'is by Al-Nasa'i

### Response 3: Even if authentic, the word 'Ahabb' (most beloved) is restricted to 'most beloved in eating this bird with me', not absolute superiority.
**Type:** DALALAH_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author rejects this restriction (takhsis) as baseless, illogical, and contrary to the absolute wording of the Prophet's prayer ('most beloved of Your creation to You'). He provides 70 distinct rational, linguistic, and contextual arguments to invalidate this interpretation.
**Evidence:** Linguistic analysis of the absolute phrase 'Ahabb Khalqika', Contextual analysis of the Prophet's prayer

### Response 4: The hadith is contradicted by the hadith 'Iqtadu bil-ladhayni min ba'di Abu Bakr wa Umar' (Follow the two after me).
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author refutes the authenticity and applicability of the 'Iqtadu' hadith through 10 specific arguments, showing it is weak according to Sunni standards and cannot override the definitive and mutawatir Hadith al-Tayr.
**Evidence:** Sunni Rijal criticism of the narrators of the 'Iqtadu' hadith

## Biographical Dossiers (5)

- **Al-Hakim al-Naysaburi** — Highly positive. Established as a supreme authority in Hadith whose authentication of Hadith al-Tayr in his Al-Mustadrak is binding.
  Sources: Tadhkirat al-Huffaz, Wafayat al-A'yan, Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya
- **Al-Tirmidhi** — Highly positive. His inclusion of the hadith in his Sunan/Jami' proves it is a recognized and relied-upon narration in mainstream Sunnism.
  Sources: Tadhkirat al-Huffaz, Wafayat al-A'yan
- **Al-Nasa'i** — Highly positive. His narration of the hadith in his Khasa'is Amir al-Mu'minin is used to destroy Ibn Taymiyyah's claim that the hadith is absent from the Sihah.
  Sources: Tadhkirat al-Huffaz, Wafayat al-A'yan, Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya
- **Ibn al-Jawzi** — Negative regarding his hadith criticism. The author compiles statements from major Sunni scholars criticizing Ibn al-Jawzi for his extreme leniency in declaring authentic hadiths as 'Mawdu' (fabricated) in his book Al-Mawdu'at.
  Sources: Al-La'ali al-Masnu'a, Various Sunni Rijal works
- **Al-Dhahabi** — Critical. The author exposes Al-Dhahabi's contradictions and anti-Shia bias (Nasb) when evaluating hadiths praising Imam Ali, showing his weakening of Hadith al-Tayr is driven by theological prejudice rather than objective hadith science.
  Sources: Tadhkirat al-Huffaz, Mizan al-I'tidal
