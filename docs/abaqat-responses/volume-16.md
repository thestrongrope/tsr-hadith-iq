# Abaqat al-Anwar — Volume 16 Response Analysis

**Hadith defended:** Hadith of Resemblance to Prophets (Hadith al-Tashbih)
**Section:** Sanad and Dalalah
**Analyzed by:** Gemini 3.1 Pro

## Overview
This volume is dedicated to defending the 'Hadith of Resemblance' (Hadith al-Tashbih), wherein the Prophet Muhammad (pbuh) said: 'Whoever wishes to look at Adam in his knowledge, Nuh in his piety, Ibrahim in his forbearance, Musa in his awe, and Isa in his worship, let him look at Ali bin Abi Talib.' The author systematically dismantles Shah Abdul Aziz Dehlavi's claim that Allamah Hilli fabricated this hadith and falsely attributed it to Sunni sources. The author provides an exhaustive catalog of major Sunni Hadith masters who narrated this tradition, accompanied by extensive biographical dossiers proving their reliability. Furthermore, he engages in a deep linguistic and rhetorical analysis of 'Tashbih' (simile) to refute Dehlavi's argument that resemblance to Prophets necessitates claiming Prophethood.

## Responses to Dehlavi (3)

### Response 1: Accuses Allamah Hilli of lying and falsely attributing the hadith to Sunni sources, claiming it is a Shia fabrication.
**Type:** COUNTER_CITATION
**Author's response:** The author proves that the hadith is widely recorded in authoritative Sunni texts by major Sunni Hadith masters and historians. He lists dozens of prominent Sunni scholars who transmitted it, demonstrating that Dehlavi's accusation of fabrication against Allamah Hilli is entirely baseless and exposes Dehlavi's ignorance of his own sect's literature.
**Evidence:** Musnad Ahmad bin Hanbal, Tarikh Baghdad by Al-Khatib al-Baghdadi, Hilyat al-Awliya by Abu Nu'aym al-Isfahani, Manaqib by Al-Khatib al-Khwarizmi, Al-Riyadh al-Nadirah by Muhibb al-Din al-Tabari, Tarikh Dimashq by Ibn Asakir

### Response 2: If Ali is equal to the Prophets in their greatest attributes, he would share in Prophethood, receiving revelation, which is absurd.
**Type:** LINGUISTIC_ANALYSIS
**Author's response:** The author uses the sciences of Arabic rhetoric (Ilm al-Bayan) to explain the rules of Tashbih (simile) and Isti'arah (metaphor). He argues that comparing someone to a Prophet in a specific attribute (Wajh al-Shabah) does not necessitate absolute equality in all respects, nor does it confer Prophethood. He proves that resemblance in virtues establishes superiority for Imamate, not a claim to Prophethood.
**Evidence:** Rules of Ilm al-Bayan (Rhetoric), Classical Arabic grammar and lexicon regarding the particles of comparison (Kaf, Mithl)

### Response 3: The concept of comparing companions to Prophets is absurd and leads to heretical conclusions.
**Type:** CONTRADICTION_EXPOSURE
**Author's response:** The author exposes a double standard by pointing out that Sunni hadith literature contains numerous reports where the Prophet allegedly compared Abu Bakr and Umar to various Prophets (e.g., Abu Bakr to Ibrahim and Isa; Umar to Nuh and Musa). If such comparisons do not make Abu Bakr and Umar Prophets, then the same logic applies to Ali.
**Evidence:** Sunni hadiths comparing Abu Bakr and Umar to Prophets

## Biographical Dossiers (8)

- **Abd al-Razzaq al-San'ani** — Highly positive. Established as a major Hafiz and a pillar of Sunni hadith transmission, proving the chains containing him are authentic according to Sunni standards.
  Sources: Tahdhib al-Kamal, Mizan al-I'tidal, Tarikh Baghdad
- **Ibn Shihab al-Zuhri** — Highly positive. Confirmed as one of the greatest Tabi'in and a central figure in Sunni hadith, validating the early transmission of the hadith.
  Sources: Tahdhib al-Tahdhib, Tabaqat Ibn Sa'd
- **Sa'id bin al-Musayyab** — Highly positive. Recognized as the foremost of the Tabi'in in Madinah, further strengthening the foundational chain of the narration.
  Sources: Tahdhib al-Tahdhib, Tabaqat Ibn Sa'd
- **Ahmad bin Hanbal** — Highly positive. Acknowledged as the Imam of the Hanbali school and a premier Hadith master, whose inclusion of the hadith in his works serves as a definitive proof against Dehlavi.
  Sources: Tarikh Baghdad, Tabaqat al-Hanabilah
- **Abu Hatim al-Razi** — Highly positive. Established as a rigorous hadith critic whose transmission of the hadith adds immense weight to its authenticity.
  Sources: Al-Jarh wa al-Ta'dil, Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyyah
- **Abu Nu'aym al-Isfahani** — Highly positive. Defended against detractors; established as a major Hafiz and author of Hilyat al-Awliya, a key source for the hadith.
  Sources: Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyyah, Siyar A'lam al-Nubala
- **Al-Khatib al-Khwarizmi** — Highly positive. Established as a major Hanafi scholar and orator, refuting any claims that his Manaqib is a Shia work.
  Sources: Al-Jawahir al-Mudiyyah, Tabaqat al-Hanafiyyah
- **Muhibb al-Din al-Tabari** — Highly positive. Confirmed as a leading Shafi'i jurist and Hafiz of Makkah, whose work Al-Riyadh al-Nadirah is a definitive Sunni source.
  Sources: Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyyah, Tarikh al-Islam
