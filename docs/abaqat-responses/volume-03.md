# Abaqat al-Anwar — Volume 3 Response Analysis

**Hadith defended:** Hadith of Ghadir Khumm
**Section:** Sanad
**Analyzed by:** Gemini 3.1 Pro

## Overview
This volume defends the Tawatur and authenticity of the Hadith of Ghadir against the argument raised by Fakhr al-Din al-Razi (and followed by later Sunni polemicists) that the hadith is weakened because early historians like Al-Waqidi and Ibn Ishaq omitted it. The author systematically destroys the credibility of Al-Waqidi and Ibn Ishaq using Sunni Rijal sources, proving they are considered weak, fabricators, or unreliable. He also proves that Ibn Ishaq actually did narrate the context of Ghadir. Furthermore, he attacks Razi for relying on Al-Jahiz, exposing Al-Jahiz as a Nasibi and refuting his anti-Ali arguments using the works of Abu Ja'far al-Iskafi and Shaykh al-Mufid.

## Responses to Dehlavi (2)

### Response 1: Hadith of Ghadir Khumm: Dehlavi accepts the hadith as authentic but completely rejects the Shia linguistic and contextual interpretation. (The author addresses the broader Sunni polemical foundation, specifically Fakhr al-Razi's claim, which attempts to deny the Tawatur of Ghadir).
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author refutes the argument that Ghadir lacks Tawatur because historians like Al-Waqidi and Ibn Ishaq omitted it. He proves through extensive Sunni Rijal citations that Waqidi and Ibn Ishaq are considered weak, fabricators, and unreliable by Sunni scholars themselves, making their omission irrelevant. Furthermore, he proves that Ibn Ishaq actually did narrate the context of Ghadir (the complaint of the army of Yemen).
**Evidence:** Mizan al-I'tidal by Al-Dhahabi, Taqrib al-Tahdhib by Ibn Hajar, Uyun al-Athar by Ibn Sayyid al-Nas, Al-Bidayah wa al-Nihayah by Ibn Kathir, Al-Sawa'iq al-Muhriqah by Ibn Hajar al-Haytami

### Response 2: Shia: No one, friend or foe, narrates any faults about Ali. Dehlavi: This is factually incorrect... the Khawarij and Nawasib fabricated massive volumes of faults against Ali. Dehlavi lists several of these alleged faults to prove they exist.
**Type:** REBUTTAL_LINEAGE
**Author's response:** The author attacks the Sunni reliance on Nasibi scholars like Al-Jahiz to undermine Ali's virtues. He quotes Abu Ja'far al-Iskafi and Shaykh al-Mufid to systematically dismantle Al-Jahiz's attempts to downplay Ali's early Islam (claiming it was just the imitation of a child), his bravery in battles, and his sacrifice on the night of Hijrah (Laylat al-Mabit).
**Evidence:** Al-Fusul al-Mukhtarah by Al-Shaykh al-Mufid, Naqd al-Uthmaniyyah by Abu Ja'far al-Iskafi, Sharh Nahj al-Balaghah by Ibn Abi al-Hadid

## Biographical Dossiers (4)

- **Al-Waqidi (Muhammad bin Umar)** — Extremely negative. Evaluated as a liar, a fabricator of hadith, and abandoned (matruk) by major Sunni scholars including Ahmad bin Hanbal, Ibn Ma'in, Bukhari, Abu Hatim, and Nasa'i.
  Sources: Mizan al-I'tidal, Tadhhib al-Tahdhib, Al-Kashif, Taqrib al-Tahdhib, Wafayat al-A'yan, Uyun al-Athar
- **Ibn Ishaq (Muhammad bin Ishaq)** — Negative. Evaluated as weak, a mudallis, and accused of lying and Qadarism by several Sunni Imams such as Malik, Hisham bin Urwah, and Yahya al-Qattan.
  Sources: Mizan al-I'tidal, Uyun al-Athar, Al-Ma'arif, Fawatih al-Rahmut
- **Al-Jahiz (Amr bin Bahr)** — Evaluated as a Nasibi, an enemy of the Ahl al-Bayt, and a fabricator of lies and faults against Ali bin Abi Talib, particularly in his book Al-Uthmaniyyah.
  Sources: Minhaj al-Sunnah, Sharh Nahj al-Balaghah
- **Al-Sharif al-Radi** — Highly positive. Evaluated as a great scholar, the most poetic of the Quraysh, and a noble descendant of the Prophet, cited to counter Sunni polemical attacks on his credibility.
  Sources: Yatimah al-Dahr, Wafayat al-A'yan, Mir'at al-Janan, Dumyat al-Qasr
