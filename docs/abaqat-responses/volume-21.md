# Abaqat al-Anwar — Volume 21 Response Analysis

**Hadith defended:** Hadith of Thaqalayn
**Section:** Sanad
**Analyzed by:** Gemini 3.1 Pro

## Overview
This volume continues the defense of the Hadith of Thaqalayn by systematically refuting the counter-hadiths Shah Abdul Aziz Dehlavi brought to oppose it. Dehlavi cited hadiths commanding the Ummah to follow Ammar, Ibn Mas'ud, Mu'adh, Abu Bakr, Umar (Hadith of Iqtida), and the Companions in general (Hadith of Nujum) to argue that the command to follow is not restricted to the Ahl al-Bayt. The author destroys these claims by exposing the fabricated and weak chains (Sanad) of these Sunni hadiths using Sunni Rijal sources, and by highlighting historical contradictions where prominent Sunni figures ignored or violated the guidance of these very companions.

## Responses to Dehlavi (5)

### Response 1: Dehlavi counters the exclusivity of Thaqalayn by citing the Hadith of Ammar ('Be guided by the guidance of Ammar').
**Type:** CONTRADICTION_EXPOSURE
**Author's response:** The author exposes the historical contradiction that prominent Sunni figures and companions, such as Amr b. al-As and Abu al-Ghadiyah, fought against Ammar and killed him at Siffin, completely ignoring the alleged prophetic command to follow his guidance.
**Evidence:** Ibn Sa'd's Tabaqat, Ahmad b. Hanbal's Musnad, Al-Tabari's Tarikh, Ibn al-Athir's Usd al-Ghaba

### Response 2: Dehlavi counters Thaqalayn by citing the Hadith of Ibn Mas'ud ('Hold fast to the covenant of Ibn Umm Abd' / 'I am pleased for you with what Ibn Umm Abd is pleased').
**Type:** CONTRADICTION_EXPOSURE
**Author's response:** The author attacks the Sanad of this hadith, pointing out weak narrators like Qabisah b. Uqbah and Abu al-Za'ra. He also highlights the contradiction that Umar forbade Ibn Mas'ud from issuing fatwas, and Uthman beat him and cut off his stipend, proving the Caliphs did not follow his covenant.
**Evidence:** Al-Dhahabi's Mizan al-I'tidal, Al-Darimi's Sunan, Shah Waliullah's Izalat al-Khafa, Al-Ya'qubi's Tarikh, Ibn Qutaybah's Al-Ma'arif

### Response 3: Dehlavi counters Thaqalayn by citing the Hadith of Mu'adh ('The most knowledgeable of you regarding halal and haram is Mu'adh').
**Type:** CONTRADICTION_EXPOSURE
**Author's response:** The author weakens the chain by exposing narrators like Ibn al-Bailamani and Zayd al-Ammi. He then exposes Mu'adh's ignorance of halal and haram by citing historical reports where Mu'adh took gifts/bribes while acting as a governor in Yemen, which Umar and Abu Bakr forced him to surrender to the public treasury.
**Evidence:** Al-Bukhari's Kitab al-Du'afa, Al-Nasa'i's Kitab al-Du'afa, Ibn Abd al-Barr's Al-Isti'ab, Ibn Sa'd's Tabaqat

### Response 4: Dehlavi counters Thaqalayn by citing the Hadith of Iqtida ('Follow those after me, Abu Bakr and Umar').
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author proves this hadith is weak, fabricated, and rejected by major Sunni Hadith masters, showing that its narrators (like Yahya b. Salama and Isma'il b. Yahya) are abandoned (Matruk).
**Evidence:** Abu Hatim al-Razi's judgments, Al-Bazzar's Musnad, Al-Daraqutni's judgments, Ibn Hazm's Ibtal al-Ra'y wal-Qiyas

### Response 5: Dehlavi counters Thaqalayn by citing the Hadith of Nujum ('My companions are like stars, whichever you follow, you will be guided').
**Type:** SANAD_DEFENSE
**Author's response:** The author dedicates a massive section to proving the Hadith of Nujum is a fabrication. He cites dozens of Sunni scholars who declared it baseless, weak, or an outright lie, dismantling every known chain of transmission for it.
**Evidence:** Ahmad b. Hanbal, Al-Muzani, Ibn Abd al-Barr's Jami' Bayan al-'Ilm, Ibn Hazm's Al-Ahkam, Al-Bayhaqi's Al-Madkhal and Al-I'tiqad, Ibn Hajar's Takhrij Ahadith al-Kashshaf

## Biographical Dossiers (4)

- **Abu Hatim Muhammad b. Idris al-Razi** — Highly positive. Acknowledged as a supreme Imam, Hafiz, and peer of Bukhari and Muslim, whose judgments on Hadith and Rijal are authoritative.
  Sources: Al-Sam'ani's Al-Ansab, Ibn al-Athir's Al-Kamil, Al-Dhahabi's Siyar A'lam al-Nubala, Al-Dhahabi's Tadhkirat al-Huffaz, Al-Subki's Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, Ibn Hajar's Taqrib al-Tahdhib
- **Abu Ahmad Abdullah b. Adi al-Jurjani (Ibn al-Qattan)** — Highly positive. Recognized as a great Hafiz and the author of 'Al-Kamil fi al-Du'afa', an indispensable reference for weakening narrators.
  Sources: Al-Sam'ani's Al-Ansab, Al-Dhahabi's Tadhkirat al-Huffaz, Al-Dhahabi's Al-'Ibar, Al-Yafi'i's Mir'at al-Janan, Al-Suyuti's Tabaqat al-Huffaz
- **Abu Hayyan Muhammad b. Yusuf al-Gharnati** — Highly positive. Praised as the Imam of Nahw, a master of Tafsir, and a leading Hafiz whose rejection of the Hadith of Nujum is definitive.
  Sources: Al-Dhahabi's Mu'jam al-Mukhtassin, Al-Safadi's Al-Wafi bi al-Wafayat, Al-Kutubi's Fawat al-Wafayat, Al-Subki's Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, Al-Asnawi's Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, Ibn al-Jazari's Tabaqat al-Qurra'
- **Isma'il b. Yahya al-Muzani** — Highly positive. Celebrated as the premier student and champion of Imam al-Shafi'i, known for his piety and deep understanding of jurisprudence.
  Sources: Ibn Khallikan's Wafayat al-A'yan, Al-Dhahabi's Al-'Ibar, Al-Yafi'i's Mir'at al-Janan, Al-Subki's Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, Al-Suyuti's Husn al-Muhadara

## Prior Rebuttals Cited (3)

- **Ibn Hazm al-Zahiri** in *Ibtal al-Ra'y wal-Qiyas*: Refuted both the Hadith of Iqtida and the Hadith of Nujum, declaring them absolute fabrications.
- **Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani** in *Takhrij Ahadith al-Kashshaf*: Systematically dismantled every chain of the Hadith of Nujum, proving each contains liars or unknown narrators.
- **Al-Bayhaqi** in *Al-Madkhal and Al-I'tiqad*: Admitted that the chains for the Hadith of Nujum are weak and broken, despite recording them.
