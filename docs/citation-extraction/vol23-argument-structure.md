# Vol 23 — Hadith al-Safinah: Argument Structure

> مَثَلُ أَهْلِ بَيْتِي كَمَثَلِ سَفِينَةِ نُوحٍ مَنْ رَكِبَهَا نَجَا وَمَنْ تَخَلَّفَ عَنْهَا هَلَكَ
> 
> "The likeness of my Ahl al-Bayt among you is like Noah's ark — whoever boards it is saved, and whoever stays behind is destroyed."

---

## Overview

Mir Hamid Husain's argument in Volume 23 follows a three-layer structure:

| Layer | Purpose | Books | Citations |
|-------|---------|-------|-----------|
| **1. Hadith Evidence** | Prove the hadith exists in Sunni sources | 169 | 225 |
| **2. Authentication** | Establish narrator reliability via rijal | 81 | 109 |
| **3. Rebuttals** | Refute Dehlavi's counter-arguments | 119 | 169 |
| **Total** | | **348** | **518** |

Evidence spans 1070 years of Sunni scholarship 
(d. 235 AH — d. 1305 AH)

## Layer 1: Hadith Evidence

**169 Sunni books** containing Hadith al-Safinah, cited **225 times**.

These are the books Mir Hamid Husain points to and says: "This hadith is recorded in YOUR sources."
The sheer number establishes tawatur (mass-transmission), making denial impossible.
