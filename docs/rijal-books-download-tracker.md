## 7. Tahdhib al-Kamal (d. 742 AH)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Arabic** | تهذيب الكمال في أسماء الرجال |
| **Author** | al-Mizzi (d. 742 AH) |
| **Volumes** | 35 (8,640 entries) |
| **Cited in** | 5 Abaqat volumes |
| **Status** | ⬜ Not downloaded |
| **Waqfeya** | [Search](https://waqfeya.net/search.php?phrase=تهذيب+الكمال) |

Al-Mizzi's expansion of Abd al-Ghani al-Maqdisi's al-Kamal — the comprehensive biographical dictionary of narrators in the six canonical hadith collections. Contains 8,640 entries with full isnads, teacher-student lists, and jarh/ta'dil evaluations. The direct predecessor to Ibn Hajar's Tahdhib al-Tahdhib. Together they form the "Tahdhib chain" — the most important sequence of rijal works in Sunni hadith scholarship.

## 9. Mizan al-I'tidal (d. 748 AH)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Arabic** | ميزان الاعتدال في نقد الرجال |
| **Author** | al-Dhahabi (d. 748 AH) |
| **Volumes** | 4 (11,000+ entries) |
| **Cited in** | 11 Abaqat volumes |
| **Status** | ⬜ Not downloaded |
| **Waqfeya** | [Book page](https://waqfeya.net/book.php?bid=3394) |

Al-Dhahabi's magnum opus on narrator criticism — "The Scale of Moderation in Critiquing Men." Contains over 11,000 entries covering every narrator who has been criticized, with al-Dhahabi's own balanced assessment. The single most important rijal work for determining narrator reliability. Mir Hamid Husain uses it as his primary weapon: when Dehlavi attacks a narrator, Mir Hamid Husain opens Mizan al-I'tidal to show what al-Dhahabi — the opponent's own authority — actually said about that narrator.
